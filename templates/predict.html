Chest Pain Classifications
TA

Typical Angina: Classic chest pain caused by reduced blood flow to the heart muscle.

ATA

Atypical Angina: Chest pain that does not meet the classic criteria of typical angina.

NAP

Non-Anginal Pain: Chest pain not caused by heart disease or reduced blood flow.

ASY

Asymptomatic: The patient experiences no chest pain.

Electrocardiogram Metrics
Normal

Heart electrical activity falls within standard parameters.

ST

ST-T Wave Abnormality: Variations in the ST segment or T wave, which can indicate cardiac stress.

LVH

Left Ventricular Hypertrophy: Enlargement and thickening of the walls of the heart's main pumping chamber.

Exercise Metrics
Oldpeak

ST segment depression induced by exercise relative to a resting state.

ST Slope

The directional slope of the peak exercise ST segment. Flat or downsloping can indicate risk.
